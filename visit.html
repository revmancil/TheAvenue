Common Questions

Visitor
Questions

We want you to feel completely prepared and at ease on your first visit. Here are answers to the questions we hear most often from first-time guests.

Don't see your question? Contact us — we're happy to help.

What should I wear?

Come as you are! Our congregation dresses in a range of styles from casual to Sunday best. There is no dress code — we care about your presence, not your outfit.

Is there anything for my children?

Absolutely! We have Sunday School classes for all age groups beginning at 10:00 AM, and nursery care is available for infants and toddlers during the 11:15 AM Worship Service. Children are always welcome in the main service as well.

Do I need to bring anything?

Just yourself! A Bible is helpful but not required — Scripture is displayed during the service. Offering is voluntary and never required of guests.

How long is the worship service?

Our Sunday Worship Service typically runs approximately 90 minutes to 2 hours. Sunday School begins at 10:00 AM and Worship begins at 11:15 AM.

Will I be put on the spot as a visitor?

Never. We do acknowledge visitors warmly, but we will never pressure you to speak, give, or do anything you're not comfortable with. You can simply worship and observe.

Where do I park?

Free parking is available in our church parking lot at 3745 Dildock Street. Additional street parking is available nearby. Accessible parking is near the main entrance.

Beyond Your First Visit

The Journey to Membership

For those called to make THE AVENUE their church home, here is how you can take the next step into full membership and ministry.

01

Visit & Connect

Come worship with us and get to know the church family. No rush — visit as many Sundays as you need to feel at home.

02

Meet the Pastor

When you're ready, schedule a brief meeting with Dr. Carroll or a pastoral staff member to discuss your spiritual journey and ask any questions.

03

New Member Orientation

Attend our new member class to learn about THE AVENUE's history, mission, values, ministries, and expectations for church membership.

04

Join the Family

Make your public commitment to membership during a Sunday worship service. Welcome to the family — you belong here.
