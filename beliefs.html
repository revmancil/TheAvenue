Our Doctrine

What We Believe

Our faith is anchored in the unchanging Word of God. These are the core convictions that guide everything we do at THE AVENUE.

Our Foundation

Faith Rooted in
God's Word

Avenue Progressive Baptist Church holds firmly to the historic Baptist faith as expressed in the Word of God. We believe the Bible is the divinely inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God — our only sufficient guide for faith and practice.

As members of the Progressive National Baptist Convention, we embrace the great truths of the Christian faith that have been held by believers across the centuries, and we are committed to passing them faithfully to the next generation.

"All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness."

— 2 Timothy 3:16

Core Doctrines

What THE AVENUE Believes

These foundational doctrines shape our preaching, our worship, and our daily life as a church family.

📖

The Bible

We believe the Holy Bible is the divinely inspired, inerrant Word of God — the complete and final authority on all matters of faith and practice. It is God's perfect revelation to humanity.

2 Timothy 3:16–17 · Psalm 119:105
✝️

God the Father

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. God is sovereign, holy, all-knowing, all-powerful, and infinitely loving toward His creation.

Deuteronomy 6:4 · Matthew 28:19
🕊️

Jesus Christ

We believe in the virgin birth, sinless life, atoning death, bodily resurrection, and glorious ascension of our Lord Jesus Christ. He is fully God and fully man — the only Savior of the world.

John 1:14 · 1 Corinthians 15:3–4
🔥

The Holy Spirit

We believe in the person and ministry of the Holy Spirit, who convicts of sin, regenerates the believer, indwells and empowers followers of Christ for holy living and effective service.

John 16:8 · Acts 1:8 · Ephesians 5:18
🌿

Salvation

We believe salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. Every person who repents of sin and trusts in Jesus Christ is forgiven, justified, and born again to eternal life.

Ephesians 2:8–9 · Romans 10:9–10
⛪

The Church

We believe the local church is the body of Christ — a community of baptized believers committed to worship, discipleship, fellowship, ministry, and evangelism in obedience to Christ.

Matthew 16:18 · Ephesians 4:11–13
💧

Baptism & Communion

We practice believer's baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith in Christ, and observe the Lord's Supper as a sacred memorial of His death and a proclamation of His return.

Romans 6:3–4 · 1 Corinthians 11:23–26
🌅

The Second Coming

We believe in the personal, visible, and glorious return of our Lord Jesus Christ, the bodily resurrection of the dead, and the final judgment with eternal blessing for the saved and eternal judgment for the lost.

John 14:3 · Revelation 22:20
👨‍👩‍👧

Family & Marriage

We believe God designed marriage as the covenant union between one man and one woman, and that the family is the foundational institution of society. We are committed to strengthening families.

Genesis 2:24 · Ephesians 5:22–33

Our Heritage

Our Baptist
Distinctives

As a Baptist congregation, we hold to the historic principles that have characterized Baptist churches for centuries. These distinctives shape our polity, our worship, and our mission.

We are also proud members of the Progressive National Baptist Convention (PNBC), sharing a commitment to social justice, evangelism, and Christian education as expressions of the gospel.

B
Biblical Authority

Scripture alone is our guide for faith and practice.

A
Autonomy of the Church

Each local church is self-governing under the Lordship of Christ.

P
Priesthood of Believers

Every believer has direct access to God through Christ alone.

T
Two Ordinances

Baptism and the Lord's Supper as practiced by Christ's command.

I
Individual Soul Liberty

Each person is directly accountable to God for their faith.

S
Saved & Baptized Members

Church membership is reserved for those who have professed faith.
